# Awesome Lifespan Extension [![Awesome](https://awesome.re/badge-flat.svg)](https://awesome.re)

_A curated[^curation_details] list of awesome resources on lifespan extension[^definition_lifespan_extension]._

[^definition_lifespan_extension]: (from Wikipedia, see citation at the bottom) **Life extension** is the concept of extending the [human lifespan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maximum_human_lifespan "Maximum human lifespan"), either modestly through improvements in [medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medicine "Medicine") or dramatically by increasing the maximum lifespan beyond its generally-settled limit of 125 years. [[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life_extension#cite_note-1)] Several researchers in the area, along with "immortalists" or advocates of life extension, postulate that future breakthroughs in [tissue rejuvenation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rejuvenation_(aging) "Rejuvenation (aging)"), [stem cells](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stem_cell "Stem cell"), [regenerative medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regenerative_medicine "Regenerative medicine"), [molecular repair](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molecular_nanotechnology "Molecular nanotechnology"), [gene therapy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gene_therapy "Gene therapy"), [pharmaceuticals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pharmaceutical_drug "Pharmaceutical drug"), and organ replacement (such as with [artificial organs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_organ "Artificial organ") or [xenotransplantations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xenotransplantation "Xenotransplantation")) will eventually enable humans to have indefinite lifespans through complete [rejuvenation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rejuvenation_(aging) "Rejuvenation (aging)") to a healthy youthful condition. The [ethical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethics "Ethics") ramifications, if life extension becomes a possibility, are debated by [bioethicists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bioethics "Bioethics"). CITATION: Wikipedia contributors. "Life extension." Wikipedia. Last modified March 25, 2025. Accessed April 4, 2026. <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life_extension>.

## Databases & Data Resources

### Human Ageing Genomic Resources (HAGR)

1. [GenAge](https://genomics.senescence.info/genes/index.html) - Database of 307 human and 2,205 model organism genes linked to aging.
2. [AnAge](https://genomics.senescence.info/species/index.html) - Aging, longevity, and life-history data across 4,645 animal species.
3. [DrugAge](https://genomics.senescence.info/drugs/index.php) - Database of 1,097 longevity drugs and compounds tested in model organisms.
4. [CellAge](https://genomics.senescence.info/cells/index.php) - Catalogue of 866 genes associated with cellular senescence.
5. [LongevityMap](https://genomics.senescence.info/longevity/index.php) - Repository of 3,144 human genetic variants associated with longevity.
6. [GenDR](https://genomics.senescence.info/diet/index.php) - Database of 214 genes associated with dietary restriction longevity effects.

### Other Databases

1. [Aging Atlas](https://ngdc.cncb.ac.cn/aging/index) - Multi-omics database for aging biology with bioinformatics tools.
2. [Aging Biotech Info](https://agingbiotech.info/) - Curated tracker of 400+ longevity companies, conferences, and databases.
3. [ClockBase](https://www.clockbase.org/) - Biological age profiling platform spanning 2M+ human and mouse samples.
4. [ClinicalTrials.gov (Aging)](https://clinicaltrials.gov/search?cond=Aging) - U.S. registry of clinical trials filtered for aging-related studies.

## Clinical Trials & Interventions

### Key Trials & Programs

1. [TAME Trial](https://www.afar.org/tame-trial) - Landmark trial testing metformin to delay age-related diseases in 3,000 subjects.
2. [NIA Interventions Testing Program (ITP)](https://www.nia.nih.gov/research/dab/interventions-testing-program-itp) - Multi-site program testing agents that extend mouse lifespan.
3. [Dog Aging Project](https://dogagingproject.org/) - Large-scale longitudinal study of aging in companion dogs.
4. [ClinicalTrials.gov (Aging)](https://clinicaltrials.gov/search?cond=Aging) - Searchable registry of active aging and longevity clinical trials.

### Notable Interventions Under Investigation

- **Rapamycin / rapalogs** - mTOR inhibitors with robust lifespan extension in ITP mice.
- **Metformin** - AMPK activator being tested in the TAME trial for aging.
- **Senolytics (dasatinib + quercetin)** - Drugs that selectively eliminate senescent cells.
- **NAD+ precursors (NMN, NR)** - Boosting cellular NAD+ levels declining with age.
- **GLP-1 receptor agonists** - Studied for potential multi-organ aging benefits.
- **Epigenetic reprogramming (Yamanaka factors)** - Cellular rejuvenation via partial reprogramming.

## Hallmarks of Aging & Mechanisms

### Key Papers

1. [The Hallmarks of Aging (2013)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23746838/) - Original nine-hallmark framework by Lopez-Otin et al. in Cell.
2. [Hallmarks of Aging: An Expanding Universe (2023)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36599349/) - Updated twelve-hallmark framework adding three new hallmarks.

### The Twelve Hallmarks (Lopez-Otin et al., 2023)

**Primary hallmarks (causes of damage):**

- Genomic instability - accumulation of DNA damage over a lifetime.
- Telomere attrition - progressive shortening of chromosome-protective caps.
- Epigenetic alterations - dysregulation of gene expression patterns with age.
- Loss of proteostasis - decline in protein folding and degradation quality control.
- Disabled macroautophagy - impaired cellular recycling of damaged components.

**Antagonistic hallmarks (responses to damage):**

- Deregulated nutrient sensing - dysregulation of insulin/IGF-1, mTOR, AMPK, and sirtuin pathways.
- Mitochondrial dysfunction - decline in mitochondrial function and increased ROS.
- Cellular senescence - accumulation of growth-arrested cells secreting inflammatory factors.

**Integrative hallmarks (culprits of the phenotype):**

- Stem cell exhaustion - decline in regenerative capacity of tissue-resident stem cells.
- Altered intercellular communication - chronic sterile inflammation ("inflammaging").
- Chronic inflammation - persistent low-grade systemic inflammation with age.
- Dysbiosis - age-related changes in microbiome composition and function.

## Tools & Software

### Epigenetic Clocks & Biological Age

1. [Horvath DNA Methylation Age Calculator](https://dnamage.clockfoundation.org/) - Original multi-tissue epigenetic clock using 353 CpG sites.
2. [methylclock (R/Bioconductor)](https://github.com/isglobal-brge/methylclock) - R package implementing Horvath, Hannum, PhenoAge, and other clocks.
3. [pyaging](https://github.com/rsinghlab/pyaging) - Python package for biological aging clock analysis.
4. [ClockBase](https://www.clockbase.org/) - Platform profiling biological age across 2M+ molecular samples.
5. [Aging Clock (curated list)](https://github.com/mdozmorov/Aging_clock) - Papers and data related to epigenetic clocks predicting age.
6. [MammalMethylClock](https://github.com/shorvath/MammalMethylClock) - R package for pan-mammalian epigenetic clocks.

### Computational & Molecular Analysis

1. [DruAge Analysis Tools](https://genomics.senescence.info/drugs/index.php) - Tools for querying drug-longevity associations.
2. [SynergyAge](https://synergyage.info/) - Database of synergistic and antagonistic lifespan interactions.
3. [Longevity Genie](https://github.com/longevity-genie) - Open-source AI assistant for health, genetics, and longevity research.
